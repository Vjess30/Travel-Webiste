Partido


The Partido area in Camarines Sur has a long and rich history that dates back to pre-colonial times.
Before the arrival of the Spanish, the area was inhabited by various indigenous groups, including
the Bicolanos Tagalogs, and Visayans.In 1573, the Spanish explorer Juan de Salcedo arrived in the area
and established the town of Naga asthe seat of government for the region. Over the next few centuries,
the Spanish colonizers continued to expand their control over the region, establishing more towns and
converting the local population to Christianity.

In 1922, the province of Camarines Sur was divided into two districts: the Partido district in the west,
and the Rinconada district in the east. The Partido district included the municipalities of Iriga, Baao,
Bato, Balatan, Buhi, Bula, Cabusao, Calabanga, Canaman, Caramoan, Goa, and San Jose.

In the years since independence, the Partido area has continued to develop and grow, with improvements in
infrastructure, education, and healthcare. Today, the area is known for its natural beauty, rich cultural
heritage, and vibrant communities.
